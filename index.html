Enterprise Strategy Artifact

Human Capital Enterprise Operating System (HCEOS)

A federated decision-support architecture connecting Demand, Supply, Production, and Resources into a single capability production engine.

PROPOSAL FOR PALANTIR FOUNDRY / AIP

The Thesis

The Marine Corps does not have a manpower problem. It has a capability production problem across disconnected domains.

We manage people (M&RA), money (P&R), training (TECOM), and demand (PP&O) in isolation. HCEOS is the layer that integrates these without replacing the authoritative systems of record.

Systemic Failure Points

  • No Demand Signal Integration (PP&O to MCRC)
  • Invisible Enterprise Workflows (Boards/Awards)
  • Disconnected Financial Constraints (P&R)
  • No Unified Risk Model for the Commandant

The "How": Architecture

Layer 1: Backbone

A standardized enterprise ontology that defines the core "Objects" of the Marine Corps.

  • Person
  • Requirement (Billet)
  • Capability (MOS/Skill)
  • Resource (Funding)

Layer 2: Domains

Ontology extensions that allow functional owners to manage their specific logic.

  • M&RA Inventory
  • TECOM Training Seats
  • MCRC Pipeline
  • PP&O Operational Tempo

Layer 3: Decision

The Application surface where GO-level trade-offs are modeled and executed.

  • Risk Forecasting
  • Workflow Routing
  • Scenario Modeling (AIP)
  • Trade-off Analytics

The Proposal for Palantir

We are not asking for a demo.

We are proposing the engineering of a federated decision layer within ADVANA/WDP. This must respect DC,I governance while providing M&RA the ability to operationalize its data.

  • Q: Can Foundry maintain separate domain ontologies under one backbone?
  • Q: How do we prevent shadow-system growth inside the platform?
  • Q: What is the technical path for Government Ownership of the model?

90-Day MVP: Prove the System

Demonstrate one cross-domain linkage (e.g., M&RA + P&R) across three interfaces:

  • Executive COP: Strategy View
  • Board Readiness: Process View
  • Awards Tracker: Workflow View

Where This Will Fail

✕ DC,I data restrictions block ingest
✕ No shared ownership of the Architecture
✕ Scope creep kills the 90-day MVP
✕ Ontology governance becomes a bureaucracy
✕ Palantir creates a vendor-lock shadow system

Capability Is Produced — Not Managed

The Marine Corps manages people, money, training, and operational demand through disconnected systems. There is no shared decision layer connecting them into capability.

HCEOS is a federated operating model that links demand, supply, production, resources, and governance—without replacing authoritative systems.

The Real Problem

Disconnected Domains

M&RA, TECOM, and PP&O operate as independent chimneys with no automated data handshake.

No Demand Signal Integration

Requirement changes in CD&I take months to manifest in recruiting or training pipelines.

Invisible Workflows

Enterprise-level decisions (Boards, Assignments) rely on spreadsheets and email "swivel-chair" processes.

No Enterprise Risk Model

We cannot quantify the readiness impact of a 10% budget cut in M&RA vs. a 10% throughput drop in TECOM.

Local Optimization

Organizations hit internal KPIs while the enterprise capability production fails.

Redundant System Growth

Failing to share a decision layer forces each DC to build their own "shadow" analytics tools.

This is not a tooling issue—it is a system design failure.

"The risk is not building this wrong.
The risk is building it five times."

Each organization is solving its own problem. None are solving the system.

Architecture

Layer 1: Enterprise Backbone

Standardized objects: Person, Organization, Requirement, Capability, Resource, Time.

Layer 2: Domain Ontologies

Specific logic for M&RA, P&R, TECOM, MCRC, PP&O, and CD&I.

Layer 3: Decision Layer

Advanced Risk modeling, Forecasting, and Workflow routing for GO-level visibility.

Note: Authoritative systems remain unchanged (MCTFS, MCTIMS, etc.). HCEOS is the intelligence layer.

Information Governance (DC,I)

Standardized Environment

Built exclusively within ADVANA/WDP environment to ensure ATO compliance and classification security.

Policy Alignment
